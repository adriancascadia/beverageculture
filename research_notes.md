# History of Bottled Water in the United States - Research Notes

## Ancient Origins
- **Roman Empire**: First documented bottled water - naturally carbonated mineral water from Apollinaris (northern Germany) transported in earthenware jars throughout the Roman Empire
- **5th Century**: Holy wells in Europe and Middle East - pilgrims collected water in ceramic flasks with unique seals for authenticity
- **Ireland**: Over 3,000 holy wells

## European Spa Era (1400s-1800s)
- **1400s-1500s**: Great European spa towns emerged
- **1572**: Foundation stone laid for spa at Rogaška Slatina, Slovenia
- **1594**: Rogaška Medical Centre opened
- **1621**: First water bottling in UK at Holy Well
- **1665**: Glassmaking began at Rogaška Slatina
- **1767**: Joseph Priestley invented artificially carbonated water
- **1783**: Schweppes company began bottling carbonated water
- **Late 18th century**: 20,000 glass bottles produced per year at Rogaška

## Colonial America & Early US Industry
- **1767**: Jackson's Spa in Boston - first commercially distributed water in America (quart for a copper)
- **1770s**: White settlers began utilizing springs (Indigenous peoples had used them for centuries)
- **1773**: Benjamin Rush investigated and reported on spring waters
- **1783**: George Washington visited Saratoga Springs
- **1786**: Benjamin Rush published 12-page booklet on medicinal benefits of mineral water
- **1800**: Waters near Albany, NY bottled commercially
- **1820**: First Saratoga Springs bottled water sold
- **1844**: Poland Spring founded in Maine (innkeeper discovered therapeutic properties)

## Major Historic Brands Still Operating
- **Poland Spring** (1845)
- **Mountain Valley Spring Water** (1871)
- **Saratoga Spring Water** (1872)
- **Deer Park** (1873)

## 20th Century Decline & Revival
- **Early 1900s**: Biggest market was pharmacies - bottled water used as medicine
- **First half of 20th century**: Americans moved to soda fountains and soft drinks
- **1976**: Perrier (French brand) began advertising in US
- **1978**: Modern American bottled water industry effectively started with Perrier launch
- **1980s-2000s**: Interest in bottled water picked up significantly
- **1994**: PepsiCo introduced Aquafina (purified water)
- **1999**: Coca-Cola introduced Dasani (purified water)
- **2016**: Bottled water sales surpassed soft drinks in the US

## Key Themes
- Health benefits and medicinal properties central to early marketing
- Wealthy elite status symbol (spa culture)
- Glass manufacturing and railroads enabled mass distribution
- Fitness craze in 1970s-80s aligned with Perrier marketing
- Shift from mineral/spring water to purified water in 1990s
